    • 'The Blue Box' Production

    By Sophie Boylan Film as Art 2026

    Initial Concept

    My Initial concept was to explore Ideas of Patriarchy through the color Ultramarine Blue and how women are represented within Art. With that I wanted to display the categorization of women through religious ideals and the entrapment they faced connecting to how women today are represented and viewed.

    Exploration
    Where to go from here

    First, I was fascinated by human form, so I decide to work with green-screen and statues. I found that this allowed me to figure out what I liked and didnt like.

    I came up with the concept of male manipulation in art and how they created yet stained societal views in art surrounding women.

    Later, I decided to put this piece of fabric over human form to ecplore ideas of male manipulation on female form

    Making The Blue Box

    The Beginning

    After my research, I came to the conclusion that I wanted to make a piece involving a girl trapped inside her own thoughts. From that I came to the idea of creating 'The Blue Box', represent womens identity and thoughts being stained and categorised by men.

    Location

    First, I tried projecting in the back of the art film base in IADT. This is where I got to test out the positioning of the projector and figure out what I wanted visually. This space did not work for me in terms of my final filming as the space was too bright.

    This was the TV 3 Studio, I found this a lot better as I could manipulate the styrofoam walls to my liking, the room was natural dark which made the projection more prominent.
